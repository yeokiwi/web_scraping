The changes to 14 CFR Part 23 primarily involve the reorganization and modernization of airworthiness standards for normal category airplanes, transitioning from a prescriptive, detailed regulatory structure to a more flexible, performance-based framework. Key updates include the introduction of new subparts (A through G) that consolidate requirements into broader categories like Flight, Structures, Design and Construction, Powerplant, Equipment, and Flightcrew Interface, with specific sections such as § 23.2000 on applicability and definitions, § 23.2100 on weight and center of gravity, and § 23.2500 on airplane level systems requirements.
