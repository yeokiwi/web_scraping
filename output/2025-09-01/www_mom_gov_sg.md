The Employment Act has been updated to include key changes effective from 1 April 2019, with the latest information reflecting an update as of 21 August 2025. The amendments focus on enhanced protections and clarifications, including expanded coverage for employees, updated definitions for managers/executives and workmen, and new requirements for Key Employment Terms (KETs) and payslips. ## Responsible re-employment

### Metadata
- **Published:** 2025-09-01
- **Link:** https://www.mom.gov.sg/employment-practices/re-employment
- **Relevance Score:** 104.5
- **Last Processed:** 2025-09-01T17:07:08.390862
- **Is New:** True

### Keywords Found
- security (content: 4)
- terrorism (content: 3)
- contract (content: 17)
- manpower (content: 12)
- employment (title: 1)
- work passes (content: 8)
- development (content: 5)

### AI Summary
The document outlines key updates to Singapore's re-employment policies, effective from 1 July 2022, including an increase in the re-employment age from 67 to 68, with a further rise to 69 planned for 1 July 2026. It also introduces a lower Employment Assistance Payment (EAP) option for employees re-employed for at least 30 months since age 63, set at 2 months' salary (minimum $4,000, maximum $8,500), alongside the standard EAP of 3.5 months' salary. Additionally, the revised guidelines encourage employers to adopt higher internal retirement and re-employment ages, implement structured career planning for employees aged 45 and above, use job redesign for age-friendly workplaces, and restructure medical benefits to include MediSave contributions or flexible benefits.
