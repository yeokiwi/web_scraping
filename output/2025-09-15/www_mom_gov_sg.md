### AI Summary
The Employment Act has been updated with key changes effective from 1 April 2019, including expanded coverage for more employees, enhanced protections for platform workers under the Platform Workers Act, and new provisions for managing employment disputes through the Tripartite Alliance for Dispute Management (TADM). Additionally, there are updated requirements for Key Employment Terms (KETs) and payslips, as well as strengthened guidelines on hours of work, overtime, rest days, and public holiday entitlements.

### DSO Relevance
Of the information provided, the following points are relevant to DSO National Laboratories as a defence R&D organisation:

### **Manpower & Employment Laws**
*   **Employment Act Amendments (Effective 1 April 2019):** DSO's HR and legal departments must ensure all employment contracts, HR policies, and payroll systems are fully compliant with these key changes. This is a direct operational and compliance obligation.
*   **Fair Consideration Framework (FCF):** All hiring practices at DSO must adhere to the FCF, ensuring fair consideration of the Singaporean workforce. This is a critical compliance requirement for a government-linked entity.
*   **Contract of Service & Key Employment Terms (KETs):** DSO is legally obligated to provide compliant KETs and maintain proper employment records for all its employees, including its large cohort of researchers and engineers.
*   **Hours of Work, Overtime and Rest Day:** As DSO's research staff may work irregular hours, especially on critical projects, the organisation must have clear, compliant policies for overtime pay and rest days for employees covered under the Act.
*   **Termination of Employment & Managing Disputes (TADM):** DSO's protocols for termination and handling any employment disputes must align with the Act to mitigate legal and reputational risk.
*   **Platform Workers Act:** While likely less relevant to core research staff, this could impact any contingent or contract workforce DSO engages through digital platforms, requiring a review of such arrangements for compliance.

### **Finance, Procurement, or Corporate Compliance**
*   **Central Provident Fund (CPF):** DSO, as a major employer, has strict obligations regarding timely and accurate CPF contributions for all eligible employees. This is a fundamental financial and compliance duty.
*   **List of Employers Convicted under Employment Act:** This serves as a public compliance risk reminder. DSO must ensure strict adherence to avoid reputational damage, which is particularly sensitive for a national defence organisation.

### **Defence, Military Operations, or Airspace/Security Policy**
*   **SGSecure@Workplaces:** **HIGHLY RELEVANT.** This initiative to prepare against terrorism and build a resilient workforce is directly applicable to DSO's secure facilities and its role in national security. DSO likely has enhanced protocols, but must ensure they align with or exceed these national guidelines. This directly affects its operational security environment.

### **General Operational & Compliance Impact**
*   **TLS Browser Support Warning (Dated May 2018):** While an old notice, it highlights the ongoing need for DSO's IT systems (especially those interfacing with government portals like myMOM) to maintain modern security protocols to transact securely. This is a continuous operational IT compliance requirement.
*   **Tripartite Guidelines and Advisories:** As a significant employer in Singapore's strategic sector, DSO is expected to be a model employer and adhere to all national tripartite guidelines on fair employment, re-employment, and progressive HR practices.

### AI Summary
The document outlines key changes to Singapore's re-employment policy effective from 1 July 2022, including raising the re-employment age from 67 to 68, with a further increase to 69 planned for 1 July 2026. It also introduces a lower Employment Assistance Payment (EAP) option for employees re-employed for at least 30 months since age 63, sets new minimum and maximum EAP amounts, and provides updated guidelines on part-time re-employment, medical benefits restructuring, and structured career planning for employees aged 45 and above.
